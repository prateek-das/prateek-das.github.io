The Impacts of Culture on Help-Seeking Behavior

By diving deep into different cultures and seeing how they view mental health, illness, and the factors that affect that viewpoint, we can learn that mainly, mental health is looked down on and is discouraged. Almost all cultures see it as a sign of "weakness" or "weirdness" and try to think the person with mental issues are at fault. However, these misunderstandings can hurt the fragile state of mind of a person with a mental illness, and, as seen in most cases, can result with devastating events.

Culture is a particular group’s beliefs, customs, values and way of thinking, behaving and communicating. Cultural background affects how someone:

  • Views Mental Health Conditions
  • Describes Symptoms
  • Communicates with health care providers such as doctors and mental health professionals
  • Receives and responds to treatment

Culture plays a significant role in shaping how individuals perceive and engage with mental health. A person's cultural background influences their understanding of mental health conditions, including how they recognize and describe symptoms. For example, some cultures may interpret emotional distress as physical illness or view mental health struggles as a source of stigma, while others might be more open and accepting. Additionally, cultural values and communication styles affect how individuals interact with healthcare providers, such as doctors and mental health professionals. These differences can influence not only how patients convey their concerns but also how they respond to treatment. Understanding and respecting cultural backgrounds is crucial for healthcare providers in offering effective, tailored care that meets the unique needs of each patient.

The images of being lazy and "worthless," the family pressures and stress of reputation, the experience of immigration, as well as the conflicting encounters with foreign colonizers and a tragic past can all play as factors to how mental health is viewed. From research, we can see that this is one of the common facts about almost any culture around the world.
